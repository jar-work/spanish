# 01 · Introduction to Spanish

> Written in English for a Danish speaker learning Spanish.

---

## Special Letters & Sounds

Spanish uses the same alphabet as English and Danish, but a few letters sound completely different. These are the ones that will surprise you.

---

### CH — like *ch* in "church"

Exactly like the English *ch*. Easy one!

| Spanish | Meaning | Sounds like |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **ch**ancho | pig | **ch**urch |
| no**ch**e | night | no-**ch**ay |
| **ch**ocolate | chocolate | *(same as English!)* |

---

### LL — like *y* in "yes"

| Spanish | Meaning | Sounds like |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **ll**ama | llama | **y**ah-mah |
| **ll**over | to rain | **y**oh-vair |
| ca**ll**e | street | cah-**y**ay |

---

### Ñ — like *ny* in "canyon"

| Spanish | Meaning | Sounds like |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| ni**ñ**a | girl | nee-**ny**ah |
| ma**ñ**ana | tomorrow | mah-**ny**ah-nah |
| ba**ñ**o | bathroom | bah-**ny**oh |

---

### H — always silent

The H in Spanish is **never pronounced**. Always. No exceptions.

| Spanish | Meaning | Sounds like |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **h**ola | hello | *oh*-lah |
| **h**ablar | to speak | ah-BLAR |
| **h**ambre | hunger | AHM-bray |

> Like the silent H in English *hour* or *honest* — but in Spanish it is **always** silent.

---

### RR — the rolled R

This one takes practice! Vibrate your tongue against the roof of your mouth.
The single **r** at the start of a word is also rolled.

| Spanish | Meaning | Note |
|---------|---------|------|
| pe**rr**o | dog | rolled R |
| pe**r**o | but | soft R — totally different word! |
| **r**ojo | red | starts with a roll |

> ⚠️ *perro* (dog) and *pero* (but) — one letter changes everything.

| Gracias | Thank you |
| De nada | You're welcome |
